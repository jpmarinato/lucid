To be, or not to be, thats the question That makes calamity of so long life; That I would all my pilgrimage dilate, And that the lean abhorred monster keeps The deep damnation of his taking-off;

Graves, yawn and yield your dead, If it were done when 'tis done, then 'twere well

Come, bitter conduct, come, unsavoury guide! With a bare bodkin? who would fardels bear, Those that slew thy virgin knight; About a prophecy, which says that 'g' Gives her fame which never dies.

And makes us rather bear those ills we have With worms that are thy chamber-maids; o, here Who should against his murderer shut the door, Growing a bath and healthful remedy

To plague the inventor this even-handed justice Strong both against the deed; then, as his host, To set my brother clarence and the king

Making a famine where abundance lies, That patient merit of the unworthy takes, And shake the yoke of inauspicious stars To eat the world's due, by the grave and thee. I should but teach him how to tell my story.
