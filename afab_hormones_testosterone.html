Testosterone

Injections

Injections are a great and standard option for taking testosterone. You can have these done weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly depending on the level of testosterone. You can do these yourself, have a loved one do it, or set up recurring appointments with the doctor who prescribed it.

Gel

Gels are applied to the skin daily. After it has dried you can swim, shower, and have contact with others. Gel does have some risks involving skin to skin contact with others, as the area you apply the gel is testosterone rich, and can rub off on others if, for example, you share a bed with your partner.

Patches

Patches are a great alternative to gels if you are worried about the transfer of your hormones on others. Patches are worn for 24 hours, and changed daily. Depending on the brand, they are known to cause allergic reactions, so it is important to test the patch before committing to this hormone delivery type.

Pills

Pills are good for people who cannot use any of the other options, but do have higher health concerns. Due to the effect of testosterone on your liver, testosterone pills are known to cause higher blood pressure, and significant liver damage. If you choose to take pills it is recommended to lower your alcohol intake, and check your blood pressure daily, as well as getting more frequent blood work to watch your liver health.
