Blockers

Testosterone is a very strong hormone, as such, you will need to take a testosterone blocker until after you have had your testosterone-producing organs removed. (if you choose to do so).

Spironolactone

This taken as a pill, usually twice per day.

Spironolactone works by both blocking the production of and action of testosterone.

GnRH analogs

This is a medication family sometimes used when Spironolactone isn't tolerated.

GnRH analogs are most commonly injected, and sometimes taken as a nasal spray.

These medications work at your pituitary gland, and cause it to shut down the signals being sent to your testicles that tell them to make testosterone. These medications are very effective and well tolerated, but can be expensive, depending on coverage.
